How AstraMeter balances your batteries

Your battery has one job: cover whatever the house is using so no power flows to or from the grid. That sounds easy — until the meter reports a moment late, the kettle switches on, or you run several batteries at once. Here's how AstraMeter keeps the grid pinned to zero, live.

The trick: your meter is always a little late

A meter reports what just happened — not what's happening right now. That tiny delay is the whole reason batteries overshoot.

What's actually happening — and what AstraMeter acts on What the meter reports — a moment later

😵 A basic battery trusts the meter

It steers by the grey line — old news. By the time the meter says “you're importing 200 W,” the battery has often already covered it, so it pushes again and shoots past. Repeat that and the grid swings back and forth: hunting.

🔮 AstraMeter sees through the delay

It remembers what it just told the battery to do and adds that back in — so it works from the green line, where the grid really is now, instead of the meter's late reading. No double-correcting, no overshoot.

Everything AstraMeter does to stay smooth

Predicting past the meter's delay is the big one — here it is alongside the rest, in plain words.

🔮

It predicts, not just reacts

Power meters report with a small delay. Instead of chasing an out-of-date reading, AstraMeter accounts for the change it just asked the battery to make — so it doesn't double-correct and overshoot.

📉

It eases in

Rather than jumping the battery straight to a new target, it ramps there at a pace the battery can actually follow, then approaches gently — no big swings past zero.

🌊

It calms hunting

If the grid starts oscillating, it recognises the back-and-forth and quietly reduces how hard it pushes — while still reacting at full strength to a genuine change like the kettle.

⚖️

It shares fairly

With several batteries, AstraMeter splits the load between them, rotates which ones work to spread wear, and steps around any that are full or empty.
